Our Story

The Journey of UKBIT

Every great brand has a story, and ours begins in the heart of the Philippines, where tradition meets innovation, and craftsmanship meets contemporary design.

2018

The Beginning

SUKBIT was born from a simple observation: the incredible artistry of Filipino weavers deserved a modern stage. Our founder, Elijah Mae Adalin, grew up watching her grandmother weave beautiful bags from abaca fibers, a skill passed down through generations.

2019

First Collection

Working with just five local artisans, we launched our first collection of 50 handwoven bags. Each piece told a story of tradition, skill, and sustainable fashion. The response was overwhelming - we sold out within weeks.

2020

Growing Together

Despite global challenges, we expanded our artisan community to 25 weavers across three provinces. We developed new designs that blended traditional techniques with modern aesthetics, creating pieces that appealed to both local and international markets.

2021

Sustainability Focus

We formalized our commitment to sustainability, ensuring all materials were ethically sourced and all processes environmentally friendly. We became proud members of the Sustainable Fashion Alliance of the Philippines.

2022

International Recognition

Our bags were featured in international fashion magazines and worn by celebrities who appreciated our commitment to authentic craftsmanship. We expanded our reach to customers in over 15 countries.

2023

Community Impact

Today, SUKBIT supports over 100 artisan families, providing fair wages and sustainable livelihoods. We've established weaving centers in rural communities, preserving traditional techniques while empowering the next generation of weavers.

"SUKBIT isn't just about bags - it's about preserving a piece of Filipino heritage, one weave at a time. Every bag carries the dreams and skills of our artisans, and connects their stories to yours."

— Elijah Mae Adalin, Founder

The Meaning Behind SUKBIT

The name "SUKBIT" comes from a local Filipino term that means "to weave together" or "to intertwine." This perfectly represents our philosophy: bringing together traditional craftsmanship with modern design, connecting artisans with customers worldwide, and weaving together stories of culture, sustainability, and style.

Looking Forward

As we continue to grow, our commitment remains unchanged: to create beautiful, sustainable bags that honor Filipino craftsmanship while meeting the needs of the modern consumer. Every SUKBIT bag is more than an accessory - it's a piece of living tradition, a statement of sustainable fashion, and a connection to the skilled hands that created it.

Thank you for being part of our story. When you choose SUKBIT, you're not just buying a bag - you're supporting a community, preserving a tradition, and joining a movement toward more conscious, beautiful fashion.
